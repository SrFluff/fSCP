Mass Amnesia

Background

The mass amnesia protocol is a more modern protocol developed to protect the world from memetic anomalies. This protocol is a more general application of the council data removal protocol to mematic information. Due to it's age, the mass amnesia protocol was developed in collaboration with council members c-0 to [DATA EXPUNGED]. This protocol was not developed until the organization's containment of the first memetic anomaly in [DATA EXPUNGED].

Core concepts

To counter the effects of mematic anomalies the mass use of amnestics must be approved. The current plan is to disperse amnestics thorugh the water supply and mandated injections. While the mass use of amnestics can be seen as dangerous, the organization recognizes that a 5-10% death toll is within reason for a mass memory wipe. During the execution of the mass amnesia protocol, all G.C.O. employees with security clearance 3 and above are to not recieve amnestics less they be affected by a mematic or explicitly, and of their own volition, request amnestics be administered. Under no circumstances are the head council to take any amnestics above level 5, any rhetoric that indicates otherwise is false and should be reported immediately.
